Story of Lulu

Lulu is a is a Scottish singer-songwriter, actress, TV personality and businesswoman. She is internationally known, but especially by UK audiences in the 1960s. Her most famous hit is the song 'Shout'.

LIFE IN PICTURES

As a teenager, Lulu toured the northern clubs with her band, "the Luvvers".

She went on to establish herself as one of the biggest-selling British female singers of the 1960s.

Read More

Did you know?

Lulu made her film début in To Sir, with Love (1967), starring Sidney Poitier, and performed the title song, which went to No. 1 in the U.S.

She also sang the title song "The Man with the Golden Gun (1974) for the world-famous James Bond 007 film.

In 2002, Lulu released an album of duets entitled "Together", featuring the likes of Paul McCartney, Elton John, Cliff Richard, Sting and Ronan Keating
